MUGUMO TREE

Sacred Tree among the Kikuyu

The Mugumo (Ficus Natalensis/ Ficus Thoningii) is an evergreen type of Fig whose wood is unsuitable for lumber or firewood and it does not need humans to grow. If the tree becomes bigger that it threatens to tip, a special sacrifice was made before young men were allowed to cut it dowm.

It was a Sanctuary where spiritual rituals were conducted and people brought food and gifts to Ngai "meaning God". It is often compared to modern-day church buildings where people can go to pray and invite Ngai, the owner and source of life to bless their families and communities with " peace, abundant food, fertile women, healthy chilren and cattle".

A close study of the sacred Mugumo Tree reveals that taking care of the tree is tantamount to taking care of the environment and that when the habitat is destroyed, plundered, or mismanaged the quality of life is undermined. It is also a symbol of fertiliy and succession, " Ituika" , the passage of one ruling generation to another.

    Specifics about the Mugumo tree;
  1. It can grow as tall as 90mtrs (295ft) in the forest or savannan.
  2. Traditionally, the tree has been used for medication, rain catchment, to hang beehives and as a sanctuary.
  3. It is also called the trees of Ngai where the Kikuyu God, choose the Mugumo trees as a place to inhabit when descening from heaven.
