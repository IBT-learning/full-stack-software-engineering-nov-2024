Tradditional Marriage

Abia state, Nigeria

Firstly, the future groom finds the lady he wants to marry and tells his parents. He is then accompanied by His parents, siblings, and close family members, and contacts the family of the bride-to-be, this is called Iku aka. A date is then set or both parents to meet.During the meeting, the groom-to-be's father will state their intention.

The second stage of an igbo Wedding is the payment of the bride price, which is then followed by the Igba nkwu. At the Igba nkwu, the bride must search for her future husband who is hiding amongst a crowd of men, then she kneels in front of him and gives him wine to drink.

Some aspects of a traditional wedding include:

  1. Blessings

  2. Dancing

  3. Leaving the parents house
