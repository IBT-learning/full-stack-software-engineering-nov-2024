My new descovery

Doro Wot is a beloved Ethiopian dish often considered the heart of traditional celebrations and feasts. This rich and flavorful chicken stew is made with tender chicken drumsticks or thighs, slow-cooked in a vibrant, spiced sauce of onions, garlic, ginger, and the iconic berbere spice blend. The stew's deep red hue and complex taste come from the generous use of nitir kibe (spiced clarified butter) and a slow caramelization process. Served with hard-boiled eggs nestled in the sauce, Doro Wot is typically enjoyed with injera, Ethiopia's tangy flatbread, making it a meal that embodies warmth, tradition, and togetherness.

Tihilo is a traditional Tigrayan dish made from roasted barley flour (known as sehilo) mixed with water or milk to create a smooth, dough-like consistency. It is often seasoned with spices and served with clarified butter (tesmi) for added richness. Tihilo is a simple yet deeply nourishing food, commonly enjoyed as a snack or light meal. Rooted in Tigrayan culinary tradition, it symbolizes comfort and sustenance, reflecting the resourcefulness and heritage of the region.
